# Description_and_Notes
#	Description: Stable isotope (carbon and oxygen) of stalagmite So-1 from Sofular  Cave, Northern Turkey. 230Th dating of stalagmite So-1 was made on  a multi-collector inductively coupled plasma mass spectrometer  (MC-ICP-MS, Thermo-Finnigan-Neptune) at the Minnesota Isotope  Laboratory, University of Minnesota (auxiliary material Table A1).  Further 230Th dating on all stalagmites was done on a Nu Instruments  MC-ICP-MS at the Geological Institute, University of Bern.  Stable isotope analyses were performed on a Finnigan Delta V  Advantage mass spectrometer equipped with an automated carbonate  preparation system (Gas Bench-II) at the Institute of Geological  Sciences, University of Bern. Precision of d13C and d18O  measurements is 0.06� and 0.07� (1s-error) respectively.

#       Abstract: A 50 kyr-long exceptionally well-dated and highly resolved  stalagmite oxygen (d18O) and carbon (d13C) isotope record from  Sofular Cave in northwestern Turkey helps to further improve  the dating of Greenland Interstadials (GI) 1, and 3-12.  Timing of most GI in the Sofular record is consistent  within �10 to 300 years with the "iconic" Hulu Cave record.  Larger divergences (>500 years) between Sofular and Hulu  are only observed for GI 4 and 7. The Sofular record differs  from the most recent NGRIP chronology by up to several centuries,  whereas age offsets do not increase systematically with depth.  The Sofular record also reveals a rapid and sensitive climate  and ecosystem response in the eastern Mediterranean to GI,  whereas a phase lag of ~100 years between climate and full  ecosystem response is evident. Finally, results of spectral  analyses of the Sofular isotope records do not support  a 1,470-year pacing of GI.
